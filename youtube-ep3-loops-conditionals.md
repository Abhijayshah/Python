## [1:00] Part 1: The 'if' Statement (Decision Making)
*(On screen: Code editor)*

**Say this:**
> "In Python, we use `if`, `elif`, and `else`. The most important thing here is—once again—**indentation**. If your indent is wrong, your logic is wrong."

```python
score = 85
if score >= 90:
    print("A Grade")
elif score >= 80:
    print("B Grade")
else:
    print("Keep practicing!")
```

## [3:00] Part 2: The 'for' Loop (Iterating over Data)
**Say this:**
> "The `for` loop is your best friend. In Python, we use it to go through lists or ranges of numbers. Interviewers often ask about the `range()` function."

**Explain `range(start, stop, step)`:**
- `range(5)` is 0 to 4.
- `range(1, 11)` is 1 to 10.
- `range(0, 10, 2)` gives even numbers: 0, 2, 4, 6, 8.

## [5:30] Part 3: The 'while' Loop (Looping until a condition is met)
**Say this:**
> "While loops are great when you don't know how many times you need to loop. But be careful—if you forget to update your variable, you'll create an **Infinite Loop** that crashes your computer!"
